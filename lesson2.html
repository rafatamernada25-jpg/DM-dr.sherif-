Key Takeaways

Definition of Digitization

The final step of digital image formation is digitization.
This means sampling the gray values at a discrete set of points, which can be represented by a matrix.
Sampling may already occur in the sensor:
  • Conventional tube camera: Image is sampled in lines
  • CCD camera: Matrix of discrete sensors (2D grid)
Important: At first glance, digitization appears to be an enormous loss of information...

Effects of Improper Sampling

Sampling leads to a reduction in resolution.
Structures about the scale of the sampling distance and finer will be lost.
Considerable distortions occur when sampling images with fine structures:
  • Moiré Effect: In 2D grids with different constants
  • Aliasing: In 1D signals and time series

Aliasing Phenomenon

When sampling a sinusoidal signal with distance slightly smaller than its wavelength, a much larger wavelength is observed.
This is a general phenomenon of signal processing — image processing is just a special case.

Sampling Process (Mathematically)

Sampling = multiplication of the continuous function with a Dirac comb ("bed-of-nails" function)
gs(x) = g(x) × Σ δ(x − r_{m,n})

The Sampling Theorem (Nyquist–Shannon)

If the spectrum is band-limited, the signal can be perfectly reconstructed from samples — provided the sampling distance is appropriate.
Simple practical rule:
→ At least 2 samples per shortest wavelength
Maximum wave number ≤ 1 / (2 × Δx)

Sampling Rate & Resolution

Sampling Rate (frequency):
• Higher rate → more accurate representation
• Minimum: 2× highest frequency (Nyquist rate)
• Common values: 44.1 kHz (CD), 48 kHz (pro audio)
Sampling Resolution (bit depth):
• Number of bits per sample
• Higher depth → smaller quantization error
• Common: 8-bit, 16-bit, 24-bit

Reconstruction from Samples

Reconstruction formula (ideal interpolation):
gr(x) = Σ h(x − r_{m,n}) × gs(r_{m,n})
Challenge: Perfect reconstruction requires the ideal sinc function → theoretically needs infinite support

Key Takeaways

  • Digitization = continuous → discrete sampling
  • Improper sampling → aliasing & moiré patterns
  • Nyquist–Shannon: ≥ 2 samples per wavelength
  • Reconstruction needs proper interpolation (ideally sinc)
  • Higher rate & bit depth = better quality (but larger files)
  • Always consider the trade-off between quality and storage
